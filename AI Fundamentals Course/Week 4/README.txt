COURSE COVERAGE - WEEK 4
=======================

Session 10: Large Language Models & Generative AI (11 slides + notes)
- Generative vs Discriminative models
- What makes LLMs large (parameters, data, compute)
- Scaling laws and emergent capabilities
- GPT architecture and decoder-only transformers
- Training stages: pre-training, instruction tuning, RLHF
- LLM landscape (Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, Llama)
- Generative AI modalities (text, images, audio, video, code)
- Prompt engineering (zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought)
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
- AI agents and tool use
- Limitations and failure modes

Session 11: AI Ethics, Safety & Regulation (12 slides + notes)
- Why AI ethics matters (real-world impact)
- Sources of bias (data, algorithmic, societal)
- Fairness definitions and impossibility theorems
- Transparency and Explainability (LIME, SHAP, XAI)
- Accountability and documentation
- Privacy in AI (data rights, differential privacy, federated learning)
- AI Safety and alignment (reward hacking, specification gaming)
- Deepfakes and misinformation
- EU AI Act (risk-based framework)
- NIST AI RMF and US regulatory landscape
- Responsible AI frameworks and auditing

Session 12: AI in Industry & Strategy (12 slides + notes)
- AI in Healthcare (diagnostics, drug discovery, personalized medicine)
- AI in Finance (fraud detection, trading, risk assessment)
- AI in Manufacturing (predictive maintenance, quality, supply chain)
- AI in Marketing & Customer Experience (personalization, analytics)
- AI in Human Resources (recruitment, retention, workforce planning)
- AI in Operations & Logistics (optimization, forecasting, automation)
- Building an AI strategy (assessment, vision, roadmap)
- AI maturity models (5 levels)
- Change management for AI
- Future of AI (AGI, quantum, neuromorphic, climate)
- Career paths in AI (6 roles)
- Skills for AI careers (technical, business, ethics)
